What Is an EOB?

An Explanation of Benefits (EOB) is a document your health insurance company sends after you receive medical care. It is not a bill. It shows what your doctor charged, what your insurance paid, and what you may owe.

What You Will Find in This Document

This guide explains each section of your EOB in plain language so you know exactly what you're looking at.

How to Read Your Explanation of Benefits

An EOB document contains your name, address, member ID, and insurance group number. It also lists the following information:

Key Numbers on EOB

Billed Amount

The original total charged for the medical service.

Allowed Amount

The amount your insurance agrees to pay based on your plan's coverage

Insurance Payment

The portion of the allowed amount that your insurance covered

Amount Owed

The amount you're responsible for that remains after insurance pays. This includes your deductible, copay, and coinsurance.

In-Network and Out-of-Network Costs

  • In-network providers: Find "network savings" box to see what you saved.
  • Out-of-network providers: You are responsible for any difference between the billed amount and the allowed amount, and the amount you owe may be higher than expected. Check the network savings line. If it shows $0, you used an out-of-network provider.

Remark Codes

Usually 2 or 3 letters or numbers. Check the EOB bottom for a description of the code. The description is a note from the health plan that explains details about the costs.

How to Check for Errors

Errors on EOBs are common. Checking yours can save you money.

  1. Check the date and service. Did you receive this care on this date?
  2. Compare your EOB to your doctor's bill. The amount you owe should match.
  3. Check the network status. Did you see an in-network doctor? Your network savings should not show $0.
  4. Look up any remark codes. The descriptions are at the bottom of your EOB.

If something looks wrong, call your doctor's billing office first. Then call your insurance company if needed.
