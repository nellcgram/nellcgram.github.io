‚ö° Quick reference

  • Standard loan period: 3 weeks for books, 1 week for media
  • Maximum renewals: 3 times per item
  • Auto-renewal: Happens automatically on due date if eligible
  • Tools needed: Integrated Library System, Phone (888-577-5275), Patron account portal

Overview

This guide covers how agents can renew library materials. Patrons can renew most items up to 3 times by web, phone, or in-person. Before you process renewals and escalate issues to a supervisor, verify renewal eligibility with the patron.

Renewal process

1 Verify patron identity

Before you process a renewal, confirm you're speaking with the account holder:

  • Ask for the 14-digit library card number or 12-digit e-card number.
  • Verify the patron's last name and their Personal Identification Number (PIN) if they know it.
  • Confirm the due date or title of the item they want to renew.
Tip: For family accounts, verify the patron is authorized to manage items checked out on the shared card. Parents/guardians can renew items for their children.

2 Access patron record

In the Integrated Library System:

  1. Select Patron Services > Search.
  2. Type the patron's library card number.
  3. Click on their account profile.
  4. Select the Account Activity tab.
  5. Review their current loans and due dates.
Important: Check for account blocks or restrictions before you renew the item. If accounts have outstanding fines over $10.00 or lost materials listed, they may be unable to renew.

3Verify item eligibility

Check the following before proceeding:

  • ‚úÖ Item has been renewed fewer than 3 times (check renewal count in system)
  • ‚úÖ There are no pending holds for this title
  • ‚úÖ Item is not reference material
  • ‚úÖ Account is in good standing (no blocks or fines over $10.00)
Important: If an item shows "Hold Queue: 1 or more," it cannot be renewed. The account holder must return the item so the next patron can borrow it.

4Process the renewal

You can renew items through two methods:

Method 1: Web renewal (patron self-service)

Guide patrons to renew online:

  1. Visit www.lapl.org
  2. In the toolbar at the left, click Books & Media
  3. Midway down the next page, click Catalog
  4. Click My Account and sign in with library card number and PIN (the default is last 4 digits of patron's phone number, but that can be manually changed)
  5. Go to Account Activity.
  6. On the left, check the box next to the item, below the word Renew. To renew all items, click the box at the top of the list next to the word Renew All.
  7. Confirm the new due date (from the renewal date). If an item cannot be renewed, a box will pop up saying ‚ÄúThe following title(s) could not be renewed‚Äù followed by a list of titles.

Method 2: Phone renewal (Interactive Voice Response System)

If the patron prefers phone renewal:

  1. Direct them to call toll-free: 1-888-577-5275.
  2. They'll need their 14-digit library card number.
  3. The automated system will process eligible renewals 24/7.
  4. The system will provide renewal confirmation.

5 Update patron case log

Document the interaction in Salesforce:

  1. Find the patron's Salesforce record using their library card number.
  2. Update their circulation case if it exists, or create a new case.
  3. Set Case Type to Renewal - Processed.
  4. Add the number of items renewed and their new due dates.
  5. Include any special notes (for example, patron education about auto-renewal).
  6. Set case status to Resolved.

Escalations

‚ö†Ô∏è Escalate to a supervisor when:

  • Patron requests renewal beyond 3-renewal maximum
  • Account has blocks or restrictions preventing renewal
  • Patron disputes that item has a hold (verify in the system first)
  • Patron requests waiver of late fees to process renewal
  • System shows error message you can't resolve
  • Patron claims auto-renewal didn't work as expected
  • Teacher card materials require special approval

How to escalate:

  1. Open a JIRA ticket with the patron's library card number and issue details.
  2. Tag a supervisor in the ticket.
  3. Tell the patron, "I need to review this with my supervisor to ensure I'm giving you the right information. May I follow up with you within 2 hours?"

Special scenarios

Items with holds can't be renewed

When there's a pending hold on an item:

  1. Explain that another patron is waiting for the item.
  2. Tell them they need to return the item by the due date or they'll get a late fee.
  3. Suggest they place a hold to borrow it again after the current hold queue fills up
  4. Offer to help them place the hold.

Maximum renewals reached

When a patron has already renewed an item 3 times:

  1. Explain there's a 3-renewal maximum policy.
  2. The patron must return the item and check it out again if there are no holds on it.
  3. If holds exist, offer to place a hold for them.

Overdue items with fines

Tip: You can still renew overdue items if they're eligible. The renewal extends the due date from the renewal date (not the original due date), which will increase the fine. Make sure patrons understand this before processing.

For overdue materials:

  1. Check if the item is eligible for renewal (no holds, under 3 renewals).
  2. Explain to the patron that renewing will extend the due date but fines continue to accrue from the original due date.
  3. If the patron confirms they want to proceed, process the renewal.
  4. Remind them to return items on time to avoid more fines.

Teacher cards (50-item limit)

To process renewals using a teacher card:

  1. Verify the patron is using a valid teacher card.
  2. Follow the standard renewal process.
  3. Some teacher materials may require librarian approval for renewal.
  4. If you need approval, tag your supervisor in a JIRA ticket.

Reporting & metrics

Renewal requests impact circulation metrics. All agents should:

  • Maintain accurate case logging for renewal requests
  • When renewals are denied, document the reasons
  • File a JIRA ticket to report system issues preventing renewals
Tip: If you notice multiple patrons confused about why auto-renewal failed, document the pattern by filing a JIRA ticket so we can improve patron communication.

Common patron questions

"Why didn't my item auto-renew?"

Response: "Auto-renewal happens automatically on the due date, but it can fail for a few reasons: someone placed a hold on the item, you've already renewed it 3 times, or your account has a block due to outstanding fines. Let me check your account to find out what's going on."

"Can you renew this even though someone has a hold?"

Response: "Unfortunately, no. When another patron places a hold, the current patron nees to return the item so the next person can borrow it next. To ensure fair access to popular materials, we can't extend loans when someone is waiting. I'd be happy to place a hold for you so you can borrow it again after the current waitlist."

"I need to keep this book longer than the 3 renewals allowed."

Response: "I understand you need more time with this material, but the 3-renewal maximum ensures materials circulate fairly to all patrons. Your best option is to return the item and check it out again immediately if it's available. If someone has placed a hold, you can add yourself to the waitlist. For extended research needs, I can also help you explore our reference collection or Interlibrary Loan options."

"Can I renew items in person at any branch?"

Response: "Yes! You can renew items in person at any of our 72 library branches or at Central Library. Just bring your library card, and circulation staff can help you at the desk. You can also renew 24/7 online at lapl.org or by calling our automated line at 1-888-577-5275."
